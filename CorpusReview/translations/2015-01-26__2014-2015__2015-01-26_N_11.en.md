The House of Representatives held a general session at 11:30 AM on Monday, January 26, 2015, which continued on Monday, February 2, 2015, under the chairmanship of Mr. Mohamed Ennaceur, Speaker of the House of Representatives, to continue discussing the contentious articles of the draft internal regulations that had been postponed.

# Opening of the session and speech by the Mr.

Speaker of the House of Representatives - On the occasion of the first anniversary of the ratification of the constitution

In the name of God, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

In God’s name, we resume our session dedicated to the ratification of the draft internal regulations. Before discussing the internal regulations, please record your attendance; we have 146 present. Let us begin our work. Before giving the floor to the Chairman of the Internal Regulations Committee, I would like to remind you that today we commemorate the first anniversary of the ratification of the constitution. It is an occasion to renew and express once again our appreciation to the National Constituent Assembly and its members for their efforts in granting Tunisia a modern and advanced constitution that guarantees rights. On this occasion, I also remind you that since the ratification of the constitution, this past year has been full of achievements and positive changes that revealed the Tunisian people's ability to overcome difficulties and their desire to belong to the dialogue to reach consensus on common problems in order to overcome the difficulties and crises that Tunisia has experienced. I want to salute the spirit of responsibility and patriotism, and I believe this will be the path forward that will enable us to overcome difficulties and to take further steps towards stability, development, and the building of the second republic.

After this speech, we would like the brother Chairman of the Internal Regulations Committee to clarify what has been reached over the past two or three days, where there was contact among various blocs to reach an agreement on the four remaining controversial articles before reaching consensus. The floor is yours, Mr. Chairman. Please proceed.
