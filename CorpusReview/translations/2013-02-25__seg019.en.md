We must clarify at this point that there are two main subgroups under semi-presidential or mixed systems, characterized by whether the prime minister and government are accountable to both the head of state and the legislative council or only to the legislative council, and also whether the head of state or legislative council alone has the authority to dismiss the prime minister or government. These systems align with presidential-parliamentary systems where the government is responsible to and can be dismissed by both the legislative council and the head of state. There are other systems called "presidential-ministerial" systems where the government is responsible to and can be dismissed only by the legislative council, meaning that under presidential-parliamentary government systems, the head of state has more political power than under presidential-ministerial systems. Some of the most established and stable democracies adopt the "presidential-ministerial" system; statistics show that stable democracies are those under "presidential-ministerial" systems, giving many advantages to the legislative council and the head of government so the system does not turn into a presidential one.
