Dialogue on the general situation in the country and the guidance document 1543

We have witnessed together the recent protest movements and the developments in several marginalized regions, which were sparked by the death of the young man, Reda Al-Yahyaoui, may he rest in peace, after which the legitimate and understandable social demands turned into unacceptable acts of violence and vandalism.\nWhile we understand the demands of our unemployed youth and their right to employment and dignity, we strongly condemn the systematic acts exploited by some disruptive parties that distorted these marches and turned them into assaults on public and private property.\nWhile condemning the exploitations that took advantage of these circumstances, we express our appreciation for the citizens, men and women, and associations who took initiative in multiple regions and contributed to protecting state institutions, sovereign sites, and public property.\nWe also renew our appreciation for the security forces and the army from various branches who dealt with the protest movements with patriotism and responsibility. On this occasion, I also express my gratitude for the security assistant Sofiane Bousseliji who was martyred during the performance of his noble national duty.\nI also commend the role played by the media in conveying the protests, dealing with them, and striving to secure the citizen's right to honest and professional information.

Ladies and gentlemen government members, dear colleagues, employment is a concern for all of us, the parliament and the government, because it is a concern of the entire people and linked to providing the means for stability in the country. But everyone must contribute to finding appropriate solutions through cooperation between the government, parties, national organizations, and civil society. The government's role remains primary as it is responsible for setting a suitable investment plan in sectors with the highest added value and employment capacity, alongside fundamental reforms in education and training curricula—a comprehensive national plan that is widely agreed upon, ensures equal opportunities for all Tunisians, and guarantees a minimum income and social protection for every Tunisian family. Achieving the revolution's goals requires patience, steadfastness, and perseverance, all relying on our unity and ability as those responsible—from the state, parliament, political parties, and organizations—to overcome our differences, interests, and visions towards what will secure progress and restore hope to people's hearts. This will foil everyone who aims to harm Tunisia, threaten our security and stability, halt our path towards betterment, and waste the opportunity to undermine our people's security, the country's stability, and territorial unity.
