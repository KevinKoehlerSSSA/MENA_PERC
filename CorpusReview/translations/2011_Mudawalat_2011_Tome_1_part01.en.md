First Council

Praise be to God

In the atmosphere of celebrating the glorious Tunisian revolution, and on the occasion of the sixth anniversary of the Revolution’s Constitution, the Assembly of the People’s Representatives is pleased to present this publication which includes the proceedings of the plenary sessions of the Constituent National Assembly related to the Constitution.

This publication, in its three parts, is an important document in the modern history of our country as it represents an effort to preserve the national memory by recording and publishing the full foundational debates that culminated in the drafting of the new Constitution, the Constitution of the Revolution of Freedom and Dignity, which enshrines the noble and major civilizational values fought for by successive generations of Tunisian men and women over long decades, in the battle of national liberation and independence, the building of the modern Tunisian state, and the December 17 – January 14 revolution. At the forefront of these values are the values of freedom, justice, equality, and the supremacy of the law.

The foundational debates have highlighted the richness and diversity of the Constituent National Assembly, and there is no doubt that revisiting them facilitates the understanding of the Constitution and opens the way for proper interpretation of its contents whenever necessary by referring back to the contexts, recalling the exceptional or specific circumstances that accompanied them, and examining the intentions pursued by the founding deputies, which the phrasing may have failed to fully convey as hoped.

The Constitution of the Revolution of Freedom and Dignity resulted from serious work and deep discussions that extended over many months, during which the deputies of the Constituent National Assembly were keen to carry out a historic mission immortalized in crystallizing the solid constitutional foundations and pillars paving the way for the second republic.

We have been keen to complete this massive and valuable documentary work and publish it at the beginning of this new legislative period (2019‑2024). On this occasion, we do not miss expressing our appreciation for the arduous efforts made by the staff of the Assembly of the People’s Representatives, without whom this document would not have seen the light in this limited time frame and with such good presentation.

This is a document of utmost importance that will surely be a qualitative addition to the legislative and legal codex of Tunisia and will be an indispensable reference for researchers in the contemporary history of Tunisia, including historians, jurists, researchers, students, and those interested in political and legislative affairs. It will also be, in the coming years, a fundamental basis for any possible review aimed at developing and enriching the original text in harmony with the ongoing changes in reality and the necessary adjustments imposed by experience.

Rached Ghannouchi
